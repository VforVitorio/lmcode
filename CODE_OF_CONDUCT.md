# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

lmcode is an open, welcoming project. Everyone who participates — through issues, pull requests, discussions, or any other channel — is expected to treat others with respect and act in good faith.

---

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and constructive in all interactions
- Give and receive feedback graciously — critique the idea, not the person
- Assume good intent; ask before assuming malice
- Help newcomers get started — we were all new once
- Stay on topic; keep discussions relevant to the project

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Personal attacks, insults, or demeaning comments
- Deliberate intimidation or threatening behavior
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Sustained disruption of discussions or contributions
- Any behavior that a reasonable person would consider inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces: GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other official channel associated with lmcode.

---

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior can be reported by opening a private issue or contacting the maintainers directly. All reports will be reviewed and responded to. Maintainers reserve the right to remove, edit, or reject contributions that violate this Code of Conduct, and to temporarily or permanently ban contributors who behave inappropriately.

---

## Philosophy

We'd rather have a great project and a good community than enforce rules. Most of this is just: be a decent person, focus on the work, and help each other build something useful.
